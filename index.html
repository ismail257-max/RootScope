Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about penetration testing and how I work.

What happens if you find critical vulnerabilities?

Critical vulnerabilities are reported immediately, even during active testing. I'll notify you via phone/email within hours of discovery, explain the risk, and provide emergency mitigation steps while we complete the full assessment. Your security comes first.

Do you test in production or staging environments?

I can test either, depending on your needs. Staging is preferred for initial assessments to avoid any service disruption. For production testing, I use non-invasive techniques and work during agreed maintenance windows. We'll discuss the best approach during our discovery call.

What if I can't afford to fix everything right away?

My reports prioritize findings by severity and business impact. I'll help you create a remediation roadmap focusing on critical/high risks first. Many fixes are simple configuration changes that cost nothing. I also provide 30-60 day follow-up support to answer questions as you implement fixes.

How do I verify your credentials and experience?

I'm happy to provide references from past clients (with their permission), show you my TryHackMe profile and certifications, and walk you through sample reports from practice environments. We can also start with a smaller scope project to build trust before larger engagements.

What's included in the penetration testing report?

Every report includes: Executive summary, detailed methodology, vulnerability findings with severity ratings (CVSS scores), step-by-step reproduction steps, proof-of-concept screenshots, real-world impact analysis, and specific remediation recommendations with code examples where applicable.

Do you offer re-testing after we fix vulnerabilities?

Yes! The Comprehensive package includes free re-testing. For Basic and Standard packages, re-testing is available at a discounted rate (typically 30-40% of original cost) since it's faster to verify specific fixes than perform a full assessment.

How long does the testing process take?

Testing duration depends on scope: Basic (1-2 days), Standard (3-5 days), Comprehensive (5-7 days). After testing, I need 2-3 business days to complete the professional report. Rush services available for urgent needs.

What makes your service different from automated scanners?

Automated tools find ~30% of vulnerabilities‚ÄîI find the other 70%. I test business logic, chain multiple weaknesses into critical exploits, and identify context-specific flaws that scanners can't understand. You get human intelligence, not just automated output.
